# Kobe and Okayama

Route from Shin‑Kobe to Okayama, visiting stations, gardens and the iconic castle.

## Shin‑Kobe Station

Shin‑Kobe Station is an important connection point in Kobe city, linking local and high‑speed trains. Its modern architecture facilitates efficient transit for travelers and tourists.

Upon arrival, the traveler senses the organisation and dynamism of the station. The excitement of starting the journey to Okayama blends with views of nearby mountains and a city that slowly says goodbye as it departs.

## Okayama Station

Okayama Station is a key node for regional transport, with a history of connectivity between Japanese cities. The station combines efficiency and modernity, serving both locals and tourists.

The traveler appreciates the speed and order in the transfer, noting the transition from Kobe to Okayama and preparing to explore nearby tourist spots.

## Okayama Korakuen

Korakuen is one of Japan’s most famous gardens, with a history dating back to the Edo period. Its design combines ponds, hills and pavilions, representing traditional Japanese aesthetics.

Strolling through Korakuen lets the traveler enjoy paths surrounded by cherry trees and maples. The sense of calm and harmony makes each step a unique sensory experience.

## Okayama Castle

Okayaama Castle, known as the “Crow Castle,” stands out for its black façade and military history. Re‑built after World War II, it remains a symbol of the city.

The visitor feels the castle’s grandeur and imagines life in the samurai era. Climbing its towers offers panoramic views of the city and the nearby Korakuen garden.
