# Osaka Essential

Welcome to Osaka, a city that blends millennia of history, temples and vibrant markets with modern skyscrapers. In this tour we’ll visit Shin‑Kobe Station, Osaka Station, Osaka Castle, Shitenno‑ji Temple and Harukas 300, enjoying urban transport and walks through its historic districts.

---

## Shin‑Kobe Station

Shin‑Kobe Station, opened in 1972, is a key Shinkansen Sanyo station linking Kobe with major cities such as Osaka and Hiroshima. Its modern architecture reflects post‑war Japan’s economic boom and facilitates mobility for both locals and tourists.

Arriving at the station immerses you in urban activity: fast trains, rolling luggage and nearby cafés create a vibrant, welcoming atmosphere. Walking across its platforms lets you feel Kobe’s energy from the very first moment.

---

## Osaka Station

Osaka Station is the city’s central transport hub, evolving since its 1874 opening into a modern complex with shopping centres and train/metro connections. It’s vital to understand Osaka’s urban expansion.

Exploring Osaka Station is stepping into an urban microcosm: shops, travellers and neon lights create a constant flow that accompanies visitors on their journey through the city.

## Osaka Castle

Built in 1583 by Toyotomi Hideyoshi, Osaka Castle is a historic and military icon of Japan. Re‑built in 1931 after several fires, it now serves as a museum showcasing the city’s and Toyotomi clan’s history. Its structure blends stone and wood with imposing walls.

Walking through the castle gardens and bridges feels like traveling back to feudal Japan: tourist chatter, cherry scent and the castle’s grandeur create an immersive experience.

## Shitenno‑ji Temple

Shitenno‑ji Temple, founded in 593 by Prince Shotoku, is considered one of Japan’s oldest temples. Its architecture reflects Korean Buddhist influences and the Asuka‑era Japanese religious tradition.

Visiting Shitenno‑ji means walking through centuries of history: temples, bells and gardens invite reflection while visitors enjoy the place’s serenity against a modern backdrop.

---

## Harukas 300

Harukas 300 is the observation deck of Japan’s tallest skyscraper (Abeno Harukas, 300 m), opened in 2014. From here you can view Osaka in 360°, highlighting its blend of modernity and tradition.

Climbing to the observatory is an exciting experience: wind, panoramic views and the sense of height create unforgettable memories as the city unfolds beneath your feet.

---

## Shinsekai Hondori Commercial Street

Shinsekai Hondori is a famous commercial street opened in 1912, originally designed to reflect Paris and New York. Today it’s known for its traditional shops, food stalls and Tsutenkaku Tower as an emblem.

Strolling along Shinsekai Hondori is a sensory journey: neon lights, takoyaki aromas and the bustle of locals and tourists create a vibrant, authentic Osaka experience.

---

## Isshinji Temple

Isshinji Temple, founded in 1718, is known for its Buddha statues made from human bones of former devotees, reflecting Japan’s tradition of honoring ancestors. The temple is a unique example of Japanese funerary culture.

Visiting Isshinji you sense a solemn, contemplative atmosphere. Each statue’s history invites reflection as you walk through the temple’s gardens and inner courtyards.

---

## Kuromon Market

Kuromon Market, known as “Osaka’s kitchen,” stands out for its fish stalls, fruit and local delicacies. Its history dates back over a century of active trade.

Wandering the market lets you taste authentic Osaka cuisine and immerse yourself in the bustling commerce, watching chefs prepare fresh dishes.

## Dotonbori

Dotonbori is one of Osaka’s most iconic districts, famous for its luminous signs and picturesque canals. Its commercial history dates back to the Edo period and it has evolved into an entertainment and culinary hub.

Walking through Dotonbori lets you immerse in Osaka’s vibrant atmosphere: takoyaki and okonomiyaki aromas fill the air while neon signs light up nights with vivid colours.

## Daimaru Shinsaibashi

Daimaru Shinsaibashi is a historic shopping centre located in one of Osaka’s busiest districts. It combines modern architecture with traditional details that attract both tourists and locals.

While exploring its corridors, the visitor experiences a mix of shopping, culture and urban design, watching shop windows, fashion stores and local Osaka products.

---

## Utsubo Park

Utsubo Park is an urban park in Osaka with roots from the Meiji era, known for gardens, sports areas and cultural events. It’s an oasis of calm within the city.

Walking through the park lets you relax among tree‑lined paths and seasonal flowers, enjoying a pause before continuing to explore the city.

---

## Negiyaki Yamamoto Umeda EST Shop

Negiyaki Yamamoto specialises in Negiyaki, a lighter version of okonomiyaki, located in the Umeda district. Its reputation is built on culinary tradition and ingredient quality.

Tasting its dishes lets visitors experience authentic Osaka flavours, enjoying live preparation and close staff attention, making food an essential part of the tour.
