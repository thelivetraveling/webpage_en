# Essential Osaka

Welcome to Osaka, a city that combines millenary history, temples and vibrant markets with modern skyscrapers. On this tour we will visit Shin-Kobe Station, Osaka Station, Osaka Castle, Shitenno-ji Temple and Harukas 300, enjoying urban transportation and walks through its historic neighborhoods.

---

## Shin-Kobe Station

Shin-Kobe Station, inaugurated in 1972, is a key station of the Sanyo Shinkansen, connecting Kobe with important cities like Osaka and Hiroshima. Its modern architecture reflects Japan's postwar economic boom and facilitates the mobility of both locals and tourists.

Arriving at the station is immersing yourself in urban activity: fast trains, rolling suitcases and nearby cafés create a vibrant and welcoming atmosphere. Walking through its platforms allows you to feel Kobe's energy from the first moment.

---

## Osaka Station

Osaka Station, the city's central transportation hub, has evolved since its inauguration in 1874 to become a modern complex with shopping centers and train and metro connections. It is vital to understanding Osaka's urban expansion.

Walking through Osaka Station is entering an urban microcosm: shops, travelers and neon lights create a constant flow that accompanies the visitor on their journey into the city.

## Osaka Castle

Built in 1583 by Toyotomi Hideyoshi, Osaka Castle is a historic and military icon of Japan. Rebuilt in 1931 after various fires, today it functions as a museum that displays the history of the city and the Toyotomi clan. Its structure combines stone and wood with imposing walls.

Walking through the castle's gardens and bridges is like traveling to feudal Japan: the murmur of tourists, the scent of cherry blossoms and the castle's majesty generate a unique immersive experience.

## Shitenno-ji Temple

Shitenno-ji Temple, founded in 593 by Prince Shotoku, is considered one of the oldest temples in Japan. Its architecture reflects Korean Buddhist influences and the Japanese religious tradition of the Asuka era.

Visiting Shitenno-ji is strolling through centuries of history: the temples, bells and gardens invite reflection, while visitors enjoy the serenity of the place in contrast to the modern city.

---

## Harukas 300

Harukas 300 is the observatory of Japan's tallest skyscraper (Abeno Harukas, 300 m), inaugurated in 2014. From here you can appreciate Osaka in 360 degrees, highlighting its mixture of modernity and tradition.

Going up to the observatory is an exciting experience: the wind, the panoramic views and the sensation of height create unforgettable memories while watching the city unfold beneath your feet.

---

## Calle comercial Shinsekai Hondori

Shinsekai Hondori is a famous shopping street inaugurated in 1912, originally designed to reflect Paris and New York City. Currently it is known for its traditional shops, food stalls and the Tsutenkaku tower as an emblem.

Strolling through Shinsekai Hondori is a sensory journey: neon lights, aromas of takoyaki and the bustle of locals and tourists create a vibrant and authentic Osaka experience.

---

## Isshinji Temple

Isshinji Temple, founded in 1718, is known for its Buddha statues made with human bones of ancient faithful, reflecting the Japanese tradition of honoring ancestors. The temple is a unique example of Japanese funeral culture.

When visiting Isshinji, you perceive a solemn and contemplative atmosphere. The history of each statue invites reflection while walking through the temple's gardens and inner courtyards.

---

## Kuromon Market

Kuromon Market, known as "Osaka's kitchen," stands out for its fish stalls, fruits and local delicacies. Its history goes back more than a century of active commerce.

Walking through the market allows the traveler to taste the authentic gastronomy of Osaka and immerse themselves in the bustle of commerce, while watching chefs preparing fresh dishes.

## Dotonbori

Dotonbori is one of Osaka's most emblematic districts, famous for its luminous signs and picturesque canals. Its commercial history dates back to the Edo period and has evolved into an entertainment and gastronomy center.

Strolling through Dotonbori allows the traveler to immerse themselves in Osaka's vibrant atmosphere: aromas of takoyaki and okonomiyaki fill the air, while neon signs illuminate the nights with vivid colors.

## Daimaru Shinsaibashi

Daimaru Shinsaibashi is a historic shopping center, located in one of Osaka's busiest neighborhoods. It combines modern architecture with traditional details that attract both tourists and locals.

Walking through its corridors, the traveler experiences the mixture of shopping, culture and urban design, observing shop windows, fashion stores and local products typical of Osaka.

---

## Utsubo Park

Utsubo Park is an urban park in Osaka with history since the Meiji era, known for its gardens, sports areas and cultural events. It is an oasis of tranquility within the city.

Walking through the park allows the traveler to relax among tree-lined paths and seasonal flowers, enjoying a moment of pause before continuing to explore the city.

---

## Negiyaki Yamamoto Umeda EST Shop

Negiyaki Yamamoto is a restaurant specialized in Negiyaki, a light version of okonomiyaki, located in the Umeda district. Its reputation is based on culinary tradition and the quality of ingredients.

Trying the dishes allows the traveler to experience authentic Osaka flavors, enjoying the live preparation and the close attention of the staff, making the meal an essential part of the tour.
