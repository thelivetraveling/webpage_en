This journey connects the island of Yakushima with the city of Kobe, exploring airports, stations and an iconic seaside park.

## Fukuoka Airport

Fukuoka Airport is a key international airport in Kyushu. Its history as a regional hub has driven connectivity and tourism in Japan, offering modern and accessible facilities.

The traveler appreciates the speed of connections and efficient logistics while waiting to board the next flight. The transition between flights feels agile, anticipating arrival in Kobe.

---

## Kobe Station

Kobe Station is a historic railway node that connects the city with various regions. Its relevance for regional and national transport has made the station vital for travelers and locals.

Walking through the station, one senses the bustle and organization of Japanese transport. The traveler experiences the efficiency and dynamism of a modern city while preparing to head to the coastal area.

---

## Meriken Park

Meriken Park, located in Kobe Bay, is an iconic space that blends modern architecture with sea views. Its history is marked by reconstructions after earthquakes and urban development.

Strolling through the park allows the traveler to enjoy sculptures, waterfront walks and the marine breeze. The relaxing atmosphere and panoramic views provide a perfect end to this segment of the journey.
